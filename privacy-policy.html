Privacy Policy

How Sovereign Payment Solutions Ltd collects, uses, and protects your personal data.

← Back to home
Effective date: 11 June 2025  ·  Sovereign Payment Solutions Ltd

1. Who We Are

Sovereign Payment Solutions Ltd ("we", "us", "our") is a company registered in England and Wales (Company No. 17276266), with its registered office at 71-75 Shelton Street, Covent Garden, London, United Kingdom, WC2H 9JQ. We are the data controller in respect of personal data collected through this website and through our business operations.

Our contact email for all data protection matters is: info@sovereignpaymentsolutions.co.uk

2. What Data We Collect

We collect limited personal data only where necessary to respond to your enquiry or to provide our services. The types of personal data we may collect include:

  • Contact form submissions: your name, business email address, company name, and the content of your message
  • Email correspondence: your email address and any personal data contained in messages you send to us
  • Service delivery: contact details and relevant business information provided by clients during the course of a service engagement

We do not collect sensitive personal data, payment card data, or data from individuals under the age of 18.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the personal data we collect solely for the following purposes:

  • To respond to enquiries submitted via our contact form or by email
  • To communicate with you regarding services you have requested or engaged
  • To fulfil our contractual obligations under any service agreement
  • To comply with legal obligations applicable to our business

We do not use your personal data for marketing purposes without your explicit consent, and we do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for their own commercial purposes.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data on the following legal bases under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR):

  • Legitimate interests — to respond to enquiries and manage business communications
  • Contract performance — where processing is necessary to deliver services you have engaged
  • Legal obligation — where processing is required to comply with applicable law

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected:

  • Enquiry and contact form data: up to 12 months from the date of receipt, unless a service engagement follows
  • Client data held in connection with a service engagement: for the duration of the engagement and up to 6 years thereafter, in accordance with our legal and contractual obligations

After the applicable retention period, personal data is securely deleted or anonymised.

6. Data Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure. Access to personal data is restricted to authorised personnel who require it for the purposes described in this policy.

7. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure — to request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances
  • Right to restriction — to request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format where applicable

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@sovereignpaymentsolutions.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk if you believe your data has been processed unlawfully.

8. Third Parties

We do not sell or share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes. We may share data with trusted service providers acting as data processors on our behalf (for example, email hosting providers), under appropriate data processing agreements. These providers are contractually bound to process data only on our instructions and in accordance with applicable data protection law.

9. Cookies

This website does not currently use analytics cookies or tracking technologies. If this changes in the future, this Privacy Policy will be updated accordingly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. The current version will always be available on this page, with the effective date shown above.

11. Contact Us

For any questions or concerns regarding this Privacy Policy or the way we handle your personal data, please contact:
info@sovereignpaymentsolutions.co.uk
